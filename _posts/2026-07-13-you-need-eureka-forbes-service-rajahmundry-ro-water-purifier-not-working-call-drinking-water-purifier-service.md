<h2 class="wp-block-heading">What to Do When Your RO Water Purifier Is Not Working</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>If your <strong>RO water purifier is not working</strong>, start with these safe basic checks before calling a professional:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Power Supply</strong>: Confirm the unit is switched on at the wall, the plug is firm in the socket, and the adaptor light is glowing. Try the same socket with another appliance to rule out a dead point or a tripped MCB .</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Inlet Water Supply</strong>: Make sure the inlet valve feeding the purifier is fully open. It can get knocked half-shut during cleaning, starving the unit of water .</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Storage Tank</strong>: If you have just drawn several litres, the tank may simply be refilling. Check if the storage tank is full but no water dispenses .</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Filter Maintenance</strong>: Think back to your last filter or membrane service. If it is months past due, a clogged filter is the likely cause of poor or no flow .</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Unusual Noises</strong>: If you hear strange sounds coming from your RO water filter, it could be due to air being pushed out of the system or a sudden change in water pressure. If the noise does not subside, call a technician to check tube connections and ensure the water tank's valve is properly closed. In some cases, the RO pump may need replacement .</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Common RO Problems Requiring Professional Service</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>1. Water Leakage</strong>: One of the most frequent water purifier problems is water leakage. Most often, it is due to loose connections of pipe connectors that need refitting or replacement. If the leakage is in the membrane, the O-ring (the soft rubber ring that stops water from seeping into other parts) might be damaged and require replacement .</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>2. Bad Taste or Odour</strong>: If you experience foul-smelling or tasting water, it may be due to chlorine presence or filters not working properly. It is recommended that you change filters yearly . The filters have an average lifetime of about 6 months, depending on usage and water quality . If the filters are fairly new and the taste persists, the system may need sanitizing .</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>3. Low Water Output</strong>: The filters are designed to filter out impurities, but contaminants may get stuck to the surface, blocking water flow. If filters are not cleaned frequently, a patina-like layer can form on the membrane—especially common in areas where water quality is poor. The solution is to get filters cleaned or replaced consistently .</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>4. Auto Shut-Off Feature Not Working</strong>: If the feature that stops purification when the storage tank is full is faulty, the machine continues operating and water begins to leak from crevices. A professional should be called to fix this immediately .</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>5. High TDS in Product Water</strong>: If TDS levels are rising, the RO membrane may be expended and needs replacement . High total dissolved solids in product water indicates the membrane is no longer effectively removing dissolved impurities.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->
